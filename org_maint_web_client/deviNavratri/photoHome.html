Summary

   Presented is a sort of pictorial insite of Adi Shakti and Her various forms. Parvati/Durga is the consort of Lord Shiva. Laxmi is the consort of Lord Vishnu, and Saraswati of Lord Brahma.
As of October 2020, this sequence of views is an emphasis on the Hindu festival of Sharad Navratri
   Legend has it that this is the celebration of the victory of the Goddess Durga after a prolonged and rather exhausting fight over the super demon Mahishasur, and death of the latter. The festival is 9 days long and the 10th day is known as VijayaDashmi or Dassera. (Dassera is the day when Lord Ram manages to kill Ravana)

The Nine Days of Navratri are celebrated with lot of fun and gaety. I am attempting to cover the aspect of the forms/avatars of the Goddess, which are shown as a sort of advance summary below:
In the subsequent pages, I attempt to capture, through my drawings, the different forms the Goddess in 'context of' Navratri
Firstly, the nine days of Navratri are divided by which deity (form of Shakti) to celebrate in order.

  • Goddess Durga (Days 1-3)
  • Goddess laxmi(Days 4-6)
  • Goddess Saraswati(Days 7-9)

Then, each of the 9 days of Navratri is representational of an Avatar of the Goddess
  1. Shailaputri
  2. Brahmacharini
  3. Chandraghanta
  4. Kushmanda
  5. Skandamata
  6. Katyayani
  7. Kalratri
  8. Mahagauri
  9. Siddhidatri

Order of Deities for 9 days of Navratri

1. Shailaputri
  • Etymology: Shailaputri literally means the daughter (putri) of the mountain (shaila)
  • About: After performing self-immolation in her form as Sati, the Mother Goddess took birth in the house of the King of Mountains, as the daughter of Lord Himalaya. Also known as Sati, Bhavani, Parvati or Hemavati, she is the absolute form of Mother Nature and the embodiment of the power of Brahma, Vishnu and Mahadeva.[1]
  • Day of worship: 1st day of Navratri
  • Governing planet: Moon
  • Mantra: Oṃ Devī Śailaputryai Namaḥ ॐ देवी शैलपुत्र्यै नमः
  • Iconography: Devi Shailaputri is depicted with two hands and has a crescent moon on her forehead. She holds a trident in her right hand & a lotus flower in the left. She rides on Nandi (bull).[2]
