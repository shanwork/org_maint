"Cricket is a Foony Game" *.

My Indian, and Mumbai roots give a logical assumption to being an ardent fan of Cricket, a ball game handed down to India by the British.
Seen, been euphoric and depressed at the ups and downs on the cricket.
  Remember the 1983 World Cup in England. Hoping against all odds, seeing them overwhelmingly fulfilled.
   The 1985 Mini World Cup. Victory assumed
  The 'famous' last ball six by Miandad against Indian at Sharjah *(2)
The Hearbreaking loss to Imran's Pakis in the last Test of a home series. Gavaskar's last test. Given out at 96. We lost by 16 runs
2003 World Cup final. Butchered by Ponting's marauders
.. BACK TO CHAMPAGNE TIMES
2011 WORLD CUP!!!

  • * Geoffery Boycott's Scot pronunciation
  • *(2) I still remember the Amul Ad 'It was Sharjah, boys, not Harjah'

I used to be a huuge fan of Gavaskar, with Kapil running a close second. I remember Mom shouting at me to 'study' and me arguing back for witnessing history. The 29th Ton against the Windies. The 30th. The 10,000th run. Seeing the beautful outswing, inswing and late swing bowling leaving many a batsman clueless.

"Poems .. Poems.. and more".

My take on the addiction to smart devices.
Thinking over, I realize am in favor of their usage; but seeing the way people have taken to them like drugs....

Enjoy your smart devices don't get obsessed by them!!

(Thanks Simon and Garfunkel for giving us this (original) immortal song which I have taken the boldness to adapt to today's malady

Hello smartphone, my new fiend,
I've come to lean on you again,
Because my weak mind’s started sleeping,
And my fingers start their twitching,
And the vision that was texted in my brain
Still remains
Within the keys of silence.

In bent posture I walk alone
Cocooned off in FB zone,
Got my sixth sense super working
Walking blind hardly looking
When my eyes were stabbed by the flash of a new invite
That flashed in Might
And touched the keys of silence.

And in whatsapp scroll I saw
Posts, photos, likes galore.
People texting without feeling,
People liking without thinking,
People checking in that none should really care
And no one dared
Disturb the keys of silence.

"Fools" said I,"You do not know
Texting like a cancer grows.
Look up and it may save you,
Accident that may break you
But my words like silent raindrops fell,
And echoed
In the texts of silence

And the people bowed and played
With the smartphone God they made.
And this God send out a warning,
“Buddy I need some charging”.
And the signs said, 'The words of the prophets are written on whatsapp groups
And facebook coops”.
And whisper'd in the keys of silence.
